What is this?

101 bold, beautiful, and necessary ideas to reimagine America — drawn from communities, artists, organizers, dreamers, and everyday people. This is not a policy paper. It's a living zine, a manifesto in bloom, a jewel-encrusted invitation to think bigger.

♦   ★   ♦   ★   ♦
The Ideas
❶
Universal Creative Education
Every public school gets a funded arts program — painting, music, film, dance — as core curriculum, not an elective.
Education
❷
Community Health Hubs
Neighborhood wellness centers — free clinics, mental health counselors, and doulas — within a 15-minute walk for every American.
Health
❸
The Green New Block
A block-by-block urban regreening program: solar canopies, edible gardens, and bioswales replacing concrete deserts.
Environment
❹
Artists as Infrastructure
Fund muralists, musicians, and storytellers through a federal Artists Corps — placing them in hospitals, schools, and transit hubs.
Arts
❺
Worker-Owned Everything
Tax incentives and startup grants for converting companies to cooperative ownership — starting with essential services.
Economy
❻
Participatory Budgeting — Nationwide
Give every citizen a direct vote on how a portion of federal discretionary spending is allocated. Democracy as a daily practice.
Democracy
❼
Free Public Transit
Make buses, trains, and light rail free to ride in every city over 50,000 people. Climate action meets economic mobility.
Environment
❽
Oral History Archive
A national initiative to record, preserve, and broadcast the stories of elders in every zip code before they're lost forever.
Arts
❾
Debt-Free Higher Ed
Cancel student debt and fund public universities through a financial transaction tax. Knowledge shouldn't have a ransom note.
Education
